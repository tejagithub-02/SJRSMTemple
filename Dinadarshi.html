Jayarama Dinadarshi

The Jayarama Dinadarshi is a revered Hindu panchang curated by Sri Jayarama Seva Mandali, offering daily astrological and religious guidance for devotees. This almanac helps align spiritual practices with cosmic rhythms, enhancing devotion and cultural heritage.

Meaning and Purpose

The term "Dinadarshi," meaning "daily guide," refers to a Hindu calendar or panchang that details tithi (lunar day), nakshatra (star), yoga, karana, and auspicious timings. Published by the Mandali, the Jayarama Dinadarshi supports devotees in Bengaluru and beyond by providing precise timings for rituals, prayers, and events like Ramanavami celebrations, ensuring alignment with Vedic traditions.

Significance in Devotion

The Dinadarshi is integral to the Mandali’s mission of fostering devotion to Lord Rama. It guides the scheduling of events like Ramayana discourses and music concerts, as seen during the 60th Ramanavami festival in April 2025. By offering data like Rahu Kalam (e.g., 3:00 PM–4:30 PM on April 22, 2025), it helps devotees avoid inauspicious periods and choose muhurtas for activities like puja or yagnas.

Sample Panchang: April 22, 2025

For Bengaluru (IST):
Tithi: Krishna Ashtami (ends ~11:30 PM)
Nakshatra: Shravana (6:45 AM–next day 8:00 AM)
Yoga: Siddhi (4:20 AM–next day 5:10 AM)
Karana: Kaulava (11:00 AM–11:30 PM)
Sunrise/Sunset: 6:05 AM/6:35 PM
Rahu Kalam: 3:00 PM–4:30 PM
Abhijit Muhurta: 11:50 AM–12:30 PM
This data aids in planning spiritual activities with precision.

How to Use

Devotees can consult the Jayarama Dinadarshi for daily worship, fasting (e.g., Ekadashi), or attending Mandali events. Available in print or digitally, it complements resources like *Srimad Ramayana Chitramanjari* and supports practices during festivals like Vaikunta Ekadashi. Check the Mandali’s website or contact us for the latest edition.
