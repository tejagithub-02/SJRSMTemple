Terah Koti Ramanama Lekhana Yagna

The "Terah Koti Ramanama Lekhana Yagna" (13 Crore Rama Nama Writing Yagna) is a profound spiritual practice where devotees write the sacred name of Lord Rama 130 million times. This act of devotion purifies the mind, deepens faith, and invites divine blessings.

What is Ramanama Lekhana Yagna?

Rama Nama refers to the divine name of Lord Rama, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, celebrated for his righteousness and compassion. "Lekhana" means writing, and "Yagna" denotes a Vedic ritual of devotion. In this yagna, devotees collectively aim to write "Sri Rama" or the Rama Taraka Mantra 130 million times, a monumental undertaking symbolizing unwavering dedication. Originating from teachings of saints like Tulsidas and Swami Ramdas, this practice is a cornerstone of Ramanama Sankirtana traditions.

Spiritual Significance

Writing Rama’s name is believed to be more powerful than Lord Rama Himself, as per the Ramcharitmanas. This repetitive act purifies the heart, fosters discipline, and aligns the devotee with divine consciousness. It is said to bring peace, remove obstacles, and grant spiritual liberation. The collective effort of 13 crore writings amplifies this sanctity, creating a ripple of devotion across communities.

How It’s Done

Devotees use special Ramanama Likhita Japa notebooks with grids to write “Sri Rama” or “Sri Rama, Jaya Rama, Jaya Jaya Rama” 108 or 1008 times per page. Individuals may write daily, while groups, like the Shri Kshetra Hebballi program, coordinate thousands of devotees. In Hebballi, over 500 participants completed 13 crore writings over months, tracked collectively. Globally, ashrams and virtual platforms facilitate participation, especially during festivals like Ramanavami.
